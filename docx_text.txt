0004: Purpose  CoverMap combines Sentinel-2 satellite NDVI with the Iowa 3-meter DEM to produce field-scale cover crop stand assessments and simplified erosion risk estimates for Iowa CCAs and NRCS advisors. This document describes every method, input, equation, and output in the CoverMap pipeline so that any agronomist, NRCS conservationist, or ISU Extension reviewer can evaluate the scientific basis of each output. Known limitations and planned improvements are explicitly documented throughout.

0145: 3. Digital Elevation Model and Slope Computation
0146: CoverMap auto-fetches the Iowa 3m Digital Elevation Model from the Iowa Geospatial Data Clearinghouse via WCS API. The DEM is buffered 50m beyond the field boundary to ensure accurate edge slope computation.
0147: 3.1 Data Source
0148: Parameter
0149: Value / Method
0150: Source
0151: Iowa 3-meter Digital Elevation Model — Iowa Department of Natural Resources
0152: Access method
0153: ArcGIS ImageServer REST API: programs.iowadnr.gov/geospatial/rest/services/Elevation/DEM_3M_I/ImageServer/exportImage
0154: Native resolution
0155: 3 meters per pixel — highest resolution publicly available statewide DEM in Iowa
0156: Native units
0157: UInt16 integer — elevation stored in centimeters. CoverMap auto-detects (mean elevation > 10,000 = centimeters) and converts to float32 meters before slope computation
0158: CRS
0159: EPSG:26915 — NAD83 / UTM Zone 15N (meters). Slope is computed in this CRS before reprojection to EPSG:4326 to prevent degree-unit artifacts
0160: Coverage
0161: Statewide Iowa. Fields outside Iowa require manual DEM upload
0162: 3.2 Slope Computation Equation
0163: Slope is computed per pixel using numpy gradient in percent (rise/run × 100). The DEM is delivered in meters (native Iowa DNR format). Slope percent is computed as: slope_pct = sqrt(dz/dx² + dz/dy²) × 100, where dz/dx and dz/dy are the horizontal and vertical gradient components at 3m resolution.
0164: slope_pct = sqrt((dz/dx)² + (dz/dy)²) × 100
0165: Computed using numpy.gradient in UTM meters (EPSG:26915) before CRS reprojection
0166: Known limitation: CoverMap resamples the 3m slope grid to match the 10m NDVI grid for per-pixel Risk Index computation. Slope values represent the mean slope within each 10m pixel. Micro-topographic variation at sub-10m scale is not captured.

0265: 6. LS-Factor and Risk Index (C×LS)
0266: 6.1 LS-Factor — Simplified Lookup from Slope
0267: The LS-factor combines slope length (L) and slope steepness (S) effects on erosion. True RUSLE2 LS-factor requires flow accumulation analysis to determine slope length. CoverMap uses a simplified per-pixel lookup from slope percent only — slope length is not computed.
0268: Figure 4 (existing). Slope% → LS-factor lookup (left) and Risk Index zone classification (right).
0269: θ = arctan(slope_pct / 100)
0270: S = 10.8 × sin(θ) + 0.03          [slope < 9%]
0271: S = 16.8 × sin(θ) − 0.50          [slope ≥ 9%]
0272: L = (100.0 / 22.13) ^ m
0273: LS = L × S                          [per pixel, numpy vectorized]
0274: Term
0275: Value / Source
0276: Definition
0277: θ
0278: arctan(slope_pct / 100)
0279: Slope angle in radians. Derived from per-pixel slope% computed from Iowa 3m DEM.
0280: S
0281: Continuous function of θ
0282: Slope steepness factor. McCool et al. (1987) piecewise formula: lower equation for slopes < 9%, upper equation for slopes ≥ 9%. Floor = 0.03 to prevent zero values on flat ground.
0283: L
0284: (100.0 / 22.13) ^ m
0285: Slope length factor. Assumed slope length λ = 100m — consistent with the ~90–110m implied by the previous lookup table (back-calculated). Unit plot reference length = 22.13m.
0286: m
0287: 0.5 (slope ≥5%), 0.4 (3–5%), 0.3 (1–3%), 0.2 (<1%)
0288: Slope-length exponent. Varies with slope class per RUSLE convention. Applied per pixel.
0289: LS
0290: Unitless
0291: Combined length-steepness factor. Continuous and spatially explicit — every pixel receives its own LS value from its exact slope%.
0292: Known limitation — slope length not computed: CoverMap uses simplified per-pixel slope lookup for the Risk Index map. For the RUSLE soil loss estimate, a single field-mean slope drives a single LS value. In both cases, slope length is not computed — true RUSLE2 LS requires the horizontal distance from flow origin to concentration point. This is appropriate for field advisory use but not for formal NRCS conservation planning.

0322: 6.3 Field-Level Concern Level — From Zone Distribution
0323: The field-level concern level (used in the CoverMap Advisory and report header) is determined from the pixel distribution of Risk Index zones. This approach is more meaningful than a field-mean Risk Index because it captures the area of the field that is truly at highest risk. Logic is applied in priority order — the first condition met determines the level.

0330: High
0331: ≥5% of pixels Critical, OR >40% of pixels in combined High/Critical zones
0332: Substantial erosion risk. More than 40% of field has marginal or failed cover on moderate-to-steep slopes.
0333: Moderate
0334: 20–40% of pixels in combined High/Critical zones AND Critical pixels <5%
0335: Variable cover across slope positions. Erosion risk elevated on steeper units but not dominant field condition.
0336: Low

0338: Cover adequate for slope conditions. Canopy development appears sufficient across the majority of field pixels.
0339: Priority order: (1) Critical fires if ≥20% Critical pixels. (2) High fires if ≥5% Critical OR >40% combined High/Critical. (3) Moderate fires if 20–40% combined High/Critical AND Critical <5%. (4) Low applies to all remaining fields.

0398: This is the most important limitation for NRCS and ISU audiences to understand.

0417: Expected relationship
0418: RUSLE2 evaluates worst-case — highest values
0419: CoverMap field average almost always LOWER than RUSLE2 for same field
0420: Practical implication: If a CCA presents a CoverMap report showing A = 47 tons/ac/yr and an NRCS conservationist runs RUSLE2 on the same field and gets 180 tons/ac/yr, this is expected — not a bug. CoverMap averages the whole field including flat toeslopes. RUSLE2 focuses on the steepest slope segment. Both are correct for what they measure. The CoverMap report disclaimer explicitly states: “CoverMap estimates field-average soil loss. RUSLE2 evaluates the dominant critical area. RUSLE2 outputs will be higher than CoverMap estimates for the same field.”
0421: 7.5 Cover Crop Erosion Reduction
0422: CoverMap v1.5 adds a 4th metric to the soil loss assessment section in all output formats (Streamlit app, CCA technical report, and producer summary). This metric quantifies the erosion protection benefit of the observed cover crop stand compared to a baseline scenario with no cover crop planted.
0423: % Erosion Reduction = (C_baseline - C_factor_adjusted) / C_baseline × 100
0424: Where C_baseline = 0.90 × residue_multiplier represents the same tillage system with no cover crop planted. Because R, K, and LS factors remain constant between scenarios, the percentage reduction simplifies to a pure C-factor ratio comparison.
0425: 7.6 Calculation Method
0426: Parameter
0427: Formula
0428: Example (No-till corn, NDVI=0.25)
0429: C_baseline
0430: 0.90 × residue_multiplier
0431: 0.90 × 0.30 = 0.27
0432: C_factor_adjusted
0433: NDVI lookup × residue_multiplier
0434: 0.45 × 0.30 = 0.135
0435: A_baseline
0436: Current soil loss × (C_baseline / C_factor_adjusted)
0437: 12 t/ac/yr × (0.27 / 0.135) = 24 t/ac/yr
0438: % Reduction
0439: (C_baseline - C_factor_adjusted) / C_baseline × 100
0440: (0.27 - 0.135) / 0.27 × 100 = 50%
